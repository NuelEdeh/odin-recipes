Egusi soup

Description

Egusi soup is a traditional dish from Nigeria. I grew up eating this delicious meal with my family. The soup is known for its rich, nutty flavor and creamy texture. Made with ground melon seeds (also known as egusi), which are first toasted and then cooked with a variety of spices, fish, meat and vegetables. Egusi soup is a hearty and comforting meal that is enjoyed by many people throughout West Africa and beyond.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup egusi (melon) seeds, ground
  • 1 pound of stock fish
  • 1/2 pound of smoked turkey
  • 1/2 pound of goat meat
  • 2 cups of leafy greens (spinach or kale), chopped
  • 1 medium onions chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 scotch bonnet peppers, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of quality palm oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. In a large pot, boil the stock fish over medium-heat for 1.5hrs to soften.
  2. In a skillet, heat the palm oil over medium heat. Toast the ground egusi seeds, stirring frequently, until golden and covered in the palm oil. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. In a medium pot, steam the smoked turkey and goat meat. Add the chopped onions, garlic, scotch bonnet and salt. for 8 - 10 mins, until the sauce is juicy. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Transfer the contents of the medium pot, to the large pot.
  5. Add the toasted egusi to the pot and stir to combine with the stock fish and meat. Gradually pour in more water, stirring constantly, until the mixture is smooth and even.
  6. Bring the soup to a simmer and let it cook for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the egusi has thickened and the soup has a creamy consistency.
  7. Add the chopped leafy greens to the soup and stir well to combine. Let the soup cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, until the greens are wilted and tender.
  8. Season the Egusi soup with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Serve the Egusi soup hot, with a side of fufu or pounded yam if desired.
